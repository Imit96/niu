Website Content Strategy
Luxury Ritual-Based African Heritage Haircare Brand

Version: 1.0
Purpose: Guide AI-powered IDE in generating consistent, non-duplicative, conversion-focused website content.

1. GLOBAL CONTENT PRINCIPLES
1.1 Brand Positioning

The website must function as:

A luxury editorial publication

A ritual education hub

A prestige ecommerce store

A cultural storytelling platform

It must not feel like a generic beauty store.

1.2 Brand Voice System
Tone Attributes

Elevated

Intentional

Spiritual (subtle, not religious)

Fashion-forward

Grounded in heritage

Confident but calm

Avoid

Generic beauty clichés

Overused buzzwords (e.g., “transform instantly”)

Medical or therapeutic claims (unless legally approved)

Repetitive sensory descriptions across products

Over-explaining ingredients

Writing Style

Editorial and sensory-driven

Structured and intentional

Minimal filler language

No slang

International-friendly

1.3 Universal Page Structure

Every page must follow this hierarchy:

Emotional Hook (Above the Fold)

Clear Value Proposition

Proof (Ingredients / Ritual / Science / Testimonials)

Conversion CTA

Trust Layer (FAQs / Certifications / Policies)

1.4 SEO & Structural Rules

Each page targets one primary keyword cluster.

No duplicate H1 across entire website.

No duplicate meta descriptions.

No keyword cannibalization between collections and product pages.

Each product must have unique ritual description and sensory framing.

Avoid repeating homepage manifesto copy elsewhere.

2. PAGE-BY-PAGE CONTENT STRATEGY
A. HOMEPAGE
Objective

Establish brand identity, communicate philosophy, and drive users to shop or explore rituals.

Required Structure
1. Hero Section

Short poetic headline (max 12 words)

Supporting line explaining ritual-based haircare

Primary CTA: Shop Rituals

Secondary CTA: Discover the Philosophy

2. Brand Manifesto

150–250 words

Cover:

Heritage inspiration

Ritual philosophy

Craftsmanship

Intentional beauty

Cultural reverence

3. Signature Ritual Collection

Feature 3–5 hero products.

Each product preview must include:

Ritual name

Functional subtitle

Emotional benefit

Texture or scent cue

CTA button

4. Ingredient Philosophy

Showcase 3–5 hero ingredients.

Each ingredient must include:

Cultural context

Functional role

Short editorial description

5. Social Proof

Testimonials

Press features

UGC gallery

6. Professional / Salon Invitation

Short section inviting stylists and stockists.

7. Newsletter Section

Position as an exclusive circle.

CTA framing example:

“Join the Circle”

“Enter the Ritual”

Never use: “Subscribe to newsletter”

3. Product Grid Card Requirements

Each card must show:

Ritual name

Functional subtitle

Key benefit

Price

Quick view option

No repetitive descriptions across cards.

4-week expectation

7. Reviews Section
8. Product-Specific FAQ

To prevent duplication and inconsistencies, the AI IDE must maintain:

3.1 Brand Voice Dictionary

Approved adjectives, tone rules, phrasing boundaries.

Each ingredient has one approved master description.

3.3 Approved Claims Database

List of legally compliant benefit claims.

3.4 SEO Keyword Map

Central map to prevent keyword overlap across pages.

Have unique sensory descriptors
